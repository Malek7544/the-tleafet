1. History & Civilizations

Tunisia Through the Ages


Tunisia was home to the Carthaginian civilization, one of the most powerful cities in the Mediterranean. Later, it became an important part of the Roman Empire, and sites like El Jem and Dougga still testify to this rich history.
The country's history spans thousands of years with influences from Berbers, Romans, and Arab Muslims

2. Top Tourist Destinations

Tunisia's Most Beautiful Places


Tunisia offers a variety of landscapes despite its small size:
  • North: Tabarka – mountains and forests
  • Central: Sousse and Monastir – sandy beaches
  • South: Tozeur – desert and dunes
Sidi Bou Said remains one of the most famous spots with its blue and white architecture.

3. Tunisian Cuisine

Flavors of Tunisia


Tunisian cuisine is a delightful blend of Mediterranean and North African flavors. Key dishes include:
  • Couscous – steamed semolina with meat and vegetables
  • Brik – thin pastry filled with egg and tuna
  • Harissa – spicy chili paste used in many dishes
Fresh seafood is abundant along the coast, and traditional mint tea is a staple beverage.
